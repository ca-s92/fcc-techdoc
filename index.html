Defining Technical Documentation

Technical documentation is an archive of information regarding the creation and application (and/or lifespan) of specific technology or product.

Using Technical Documentation

It can be used to inform internal teams or for users. Technical documentation are not industry specific, or only created by developers. Technical documents are one size fits all. For users, technical documentation can be more of an how-to. When used internally, documentation can be more in depth to give great detail, use cases, answers to common problems, or record the state of a product or technology currently under development.

Bit.ai blog has a great breakdown of the type of audiences and uses of technical documentation:

  • End-user support: This is for the customer or user and helps them understand the application or how to build a product.
  • Marketing support: Used by internal teams on how to market the product or technology.
  • Development support: Used by development teams to go in depth on the creation, function, and application of the product or technology. This would be a dev-heavy resource.
  • Organization support: Another internal document, a quick reference guide for employees to support them and their position.
